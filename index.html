About Me

I am a final-year Aerospace Engineering student at the University of Hertfordshire with a specialisation in Pilot Studies. My academic journey has equipped me with comprehensive knowledge in aerodynamics, propulsion systems, flight testing, and aircraft design.

My research focuses on sustainable aviation technologies, particularly hybrid-hydrogen propulsion systems for regional aircraft. I am committed to contributing to the aviation industry's goal of achieving net-zero emissions by 2050.

Academic Projects

Hybrid-Hydrogen Powered Regional Aircraft

Module: Aerospace Vehicle Design | March 2025

Optimisation of a 50-seat hybrid-hydrogen powered regional aircraft designed for 1000 nautical miles range. The project integrates hydrogen fuel cell technology (1 MW PEM fuel cells) with solid-state battery systems (900 Wh/kg) and MagniX Magni650 electric motors for sustainable aviation.

  • Twin-motor electric configuration with 6-blade carbon composite propellers
  • Complete performance analysis: take-off, climb, cruise, and landing phases
  • Failure scenario analysis with redundancy design for safety compliance
  • Zero CO2 emissions aligned with ICAO 2050 net-zero goals

Cessna 172 Skyhawk Flight Testing

Module: Flight Testing (6ENT1166-0901)

Comprehensive investigation of stall and take-off performance characteristics of the Cessna 172 Skyhawk using X-Plane 11 simulation. The study analysed stall speeds at different aircraft masses and take-off distances under varying flap configurations.

  • Stall speed analysis at three weight configurations (1157kg, 1100kg, 1040kg)
  • Take-off performance vs flap settings (0, 10, 20 degrees)
  • Optimal 10-degree flap setting identified for shortest take-off distance
  • Compliance with FAA and ICAO flight testing regulations

Low-Cambered Aerofoil Wind Tunnel Analysis

Module: Fluids and Aerodynamics (5ENT1128-0901)

Experimental wind tunnel analysis of an unknown low-cambered aerofoil using the AF1300 Subsonic Wind Tunnel. Derived coefficient of lift (CL) from coefficient of pressure (Cp) measurements at various angles of attack and Reynolds numbers.

  • Wind tunnel testing at Reynolds numbers: 129,462 to 241,519
  • Pressure tapping measurements across 20 points on aerofoil surfaces
  • XFLR5 computational validation of experimental results
  • RMSD analysis confirming data accuracy (0.2 deviation)

Aircraft Cannon Targeting System Reliability Analysis

Module: Integrated Systems Reliability (6ENT2057-0105)

Comprehensive reliability analysis of an Aircraft Cannon Targeting System (ACTS) comprising 11 subsystems. Evaluated three system configurations including series arrangement, full active redundancy, and standby redundancy for servo controllers.

  • Reliability calculations at 10 and 400 flying hours
  • Fault Tree Analysis (FTA) for failure mode identification
  • MTBF improvement analysis (80% enhancement evaluation)
  • Redundancy vs mature product trade-off analysis
